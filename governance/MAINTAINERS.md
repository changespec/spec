## How to become a maintainer

Any consistent contributor to the ChangeSpec specification, reference implementations, or conformance suite may be nominated by an existing maintainer. To be nominated:

- You have submitted at least 5 merged pull requests, or have led at least one Specification Extension Proposal through the full process.
- You have demonstrated understanding of the specification and its design rationale.
- You are available for ongoing review and maintenance responsibilities.
- You agree to abide by the Code of Conduct and Governance documents.

Nominations are discussed in a GitHub Discussion thread (labeled `maintainer-nomination`) and are accepted by rough consensus of the existing maintainer group after a 7-day comment period.

## Maintainer responsibilities

- Review pull requests within 5 business days where possible.
- Participate in SEP discussions and help the community reach rough consensus.
- Enforce the Code of Conduct.
- Cut releases following the release process in GOVERNANCE.md.
- Respond to security reports per the Security policy.
- Disclose commercial interests in any communication made on behalf of the project.

## Stepping down

Maintainers who wish to step down may do so at any time by opening a pull request to move their name to the emeritus list. If a maintainer becomes inactive (no pull request review or governance participation for 6 months), another maintainer may raise a discussion about moving them to emeritus status. The inactive maintainer has 14 days to respond before the change is made.
